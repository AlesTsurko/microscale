microscale is an interactive album and instrument made by Ales Tsurko. microscale uses random texts from Wikipedia and regular expressions to generate music.

The album called “microscale” because of the pattern matchings that are meaning's microscale level. Music is generating from this small pieces of text. The pieces form not only new meaning (the musical form), but another form of existence.
First of all it's a musical piece that you can just listen. The music which borns right in front of you. But it's also open for interpretation, so bellow is instructions for those, who wants to create his own version (or to perform microscale live).

microscale uses regular expressions to find matchings in the text buffers, which are randomly chosen articles from Wikipedia. If you don't familiar with regular expressions, here is a short course.

You can choose track by clicking on a track number on top of the text buffers area. There is a sample map for each track (the bottom-right area). The map is a table of relationships between matchings and samples. When the playing cursor achieves the match from the map, microscale plays appropriate sample. You can enter your own expression in the field. Fill free to play.
