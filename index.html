microscale is an interactive album and instrument made by Ales Tsurko. microscale uses text and regular expressions to generate music.

Independent parts of a structure forms not only new meaning, but another form of existence and at the same time it's all forming in the brain as a concept. It's showing that for the concept there is no any materials, as well as for the art there is no difference how it was created, and the only important is what an artwork brings out in the brain.
First of all it's a musical piece that you can just listen. The music which borns right in front of you. But it's also open for interpretation, so bellow is instructions for those, who wants to create his own version (or to perform microscale live).

microscale uses regular expressions to find matchings in the text buffers. If you don't familiar with regular expressions, here is a short course.

You can choose track by clicking on a track number on top of the text buffers area. There is a sample map for each track (the bottom-right area). The map is a table of relationships between matchings and samples. When the playing cursor achieves the match from the map, microscale plays appropriate sample. You can use your own text in the buffers and your own regular expressions. Fill free to play.
